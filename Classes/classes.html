What Is Yoga?

Yoga is a sssholistic mind and body practice that originated in ancient India. The word comes from the Sanskrit root yuj, meaning "to yoke," "to join," or "to unite". While it began as a spiritual discipline, in the Western world, it is most commonly practiced for its physical postures and mental wellness benefits.

Yoga benefits:

  • Improves flexibility, balance, and strength: The various postures (asanas) in yoga stretch and strengthen muscles, increase range of motion, and enhance balance.

  • Relieves pain: Gentle stretching and strengthening exercises can help alleviate chronic pain conditions such as lower back pain, arthritis, and headaches.

  • Enhances heart and circulatory health: Yoga can lower blood pressure, reduce inflammation, and improve circulation, all of which benefit the cardiovascular system.





  What is Zumba?

     Zumba is a dance-based fitness program that uses Latin and international music to create a dynamic and fun      aerobic workout. Founded in the 1990s by Colombian dancer and choreographer Beto Pérez, Zumba classes      known for their high-energy, "dance-party" atmosphere and simple, easy-to-follow choreography.

   Zumba benifits:

Zumba is a fitness program that combines dance and aerobic movements. It can provide a workout for the body and mind. A single session can burn a substantial number of calories, which aid weight managment.

   









Whta is strength training?

Strength training, also known as resistance training, is a type of aexercise that increases muscle strength by causing muscles to contract against an external resistance. This resistance can come from various sources, including your own body weight, free weights, resistance bands, weight machines, or household objects. Unlike aerobic exercise, which focuses on cardiovascular endurance, strength training is primarily an aanaerobic activity that stresses muscles to the point of fatigue, prompting them to adapt and grow stronger over time.

Strength training benifits:

  • Builds and preserves muscle mass: Muscle mass naturally decreases with age, but strength training helps reverse this trend, making daily tasks easier.

  • Increases metabolism: Building muscle increases your resting metabolic rate, meaning your body burns more calories even when at rest. This can weight management and fat loss, particularly abdominal fat.
